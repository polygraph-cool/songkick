The unlikely odds of

Making it big

Sifting through three years and over 80,000 concerts in NYC

By Russell Goldenberg & Dan Kopf

On November 3, 2012, the band Lake Street Dive was the third act at the Rockwood Music Hall, one of over 7,000 bands to open a show in the NYC area that year. The 200 person capacity show was headlined by Wes Hutchison, and Noah Gunderson was second bill. “Blah blah blah,” wrote XXX, “Blah Blah Blah.”

Four years later, Lake Street Dive headlined Radio City Music Hall. The band played the hallowed 6,000 person capacity to a near sellout crowd. There could be little doubt that Lake Street Dive had “made it”.

“Veggies es bonus vobis, proinde vos postulo essum magis kohlrabi welsh onion daikon amaranth tatsoi tomatillo melon azuki bean garlic.” says lead singer Rachel Price.

Lake Street Dive was one of over 8,000 acts that opened a concert at a small venue in the New York City area in 2013. Of those, nearly half would not return to the area to play a show from 2014-2016 (through October).

Some bands do make progress. About 3,000 of those bands go on to headline a show, and 300 of them headlined a show at venue with capacity of more than 700.

But only 21 bands accomplish Lake Street Dive’s level of making it: headlining a show over 3,000 capacity. Barring a Kanye West sized ego, any act that headlines places like Kings Theatre and Terminal 5 have reached a level of success that they could barely have dreamed of when they got started.
